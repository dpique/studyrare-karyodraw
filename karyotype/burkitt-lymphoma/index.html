Burkitt lymphoma t(8;14)

46,XX,t(8;14)(q24;q32)

Also known as: MYC-IGH, t(8;14)

46,XX,t(8;14)(q24;q32) is the classic translocation of Burkitt lymphoma. It places the MYC oncogene on chromosome 8 next to the immunoglobulin heavy-chain locus IGH on chromosome 14, driving constant MYC expression.

What the notation means

46
total chromosome count (the normal human number)
XX
sex chromosomes: two X (usual female karyotype)
t(8;14)(q24;q32)
a reciprocal TRANSLOCATION: chromosomes 8 and 14 break (at 8q24 and 14q32) and swap the pieces beyond those breaks, giving two derivative chromosomes der(8) and der(14)

Clinical notes

t(8;14), Burkitt lymphoma

t(8;14)(q24;q32) places MYC next to the IGH enhancer → MYC overexpression. Classic 'starry-sky' Burkitt lymphoma.
