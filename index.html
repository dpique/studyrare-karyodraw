About KaryoDraw

KaryoDraw is a free, browser-based karyotype visualizer for notation written in ISCN 2024, the current International System for Human Cytogenomic Nomenclature. Type a designation such as 47,XX,+21 or 46,XY,t(9;22)(q34;q11.2) and KaryoDraw draws a correctly Giemsa-banded karyogram, aligns any derivative chromosomes against their normal homologs, and decodes every symbol in the nomenclature into plain language. It runs in your browser, with no account and nothing to install.

What is ISCN?

ISCN is the standardized shorthand cytogeneticists use to describe chromosome number and structure: the arm (p or q), region, band and sub-band coordinates, and operators such as t (translocation), del (deletion), dup, inv, and i (isochromosome). KaryoDraw takes this notation as input and turns it into a picture plus an explanation, so you can check that a written karyotype means what you think it means.

Who it's for

KaryoDraw is built for genetic counseling and medical genetics learners — students preparing for ABGC or ABMGG board exams, residents and fellows, and anyone teaching cytogenetics who wants a fast way to see a karyotype drawn with the reasoning spelled out. It is an educational visualizer of cytogenetic nomenclature, not a diagnostic tool.

How to use it

Enter a karyotype in the box at the top of the page (or pick one of the examples), then switch between highlight and realistic banding, adjust the band resolution from roughly 400 to 850 bands, and hover any band to read its coordinate one digit at a time along with the biology of its stain. Every view has a shareable link and can be exported as an image or a one-page printable summary.
